Solutions Engineer

The pilot that's about
to tell the wrong story.

A mid-market e-commerce deal is one week into a pilot with a business review in 23 days. The signals from Gong and Fullstory sessions are contradicting each other — and pointing to a story most SEs would miss.

⚠ Champion isolated — no data team on any call
⚠ Session behavior contradicts stated use case interest
▲ 23-day window to business review
✓ High engagement on session replay — 4 logins, 3 replays
The Situation
Gong — Discovery Call Transcript
› Champion said "we love session replay" but expressed doubt the CTO would see ROI on the funnel analytics piece.
› Champion went quiet when pricing came up. No one from the data team has joined any call.
Fullstory — Pilot Session Data
› Champion logged in 4 times. Replayed the same rage-click session on the checkout page 3 times.
› Has not touched the funnel builder once.
Context
› Business review in 23 days. Deal value: $2M ARR.
› Customer has also requested expanding the pilot scope to a second use case.

Prompt fed to WGO frameworkRead design-system/Worlds_Greatest_Operator.md and apply the WGO framework. Then: I'm a Solutions Engineer. A mid-market prospect (B2C e-commerce, ~$2M ARR deal) just completed week one of their pilot. Here's what I know: - Gong transcript: Champion said "we love the session replay but I'm not sure our CTO will see the ROI on the funnel analytics piece." Champion went quiet when pricing came up. No one from the data team has joined a call yet. - Fullstory pilot sessions: The champion has logged in 4 times. They replayed the same rage-click session on their checkout page three times. They have not touched the funnel builder. - Timeline: Business review is in 23 days. Using the WGO SE Playbook, the Gong reading protocol, and the 24h/30-90d model — give me the three decisions I need to make this week, ranked by impact. Not a task list. Decisions.
WGO Output — 3 Decisions, Ranked by Impact
Pivot Decision 1 of 3
Reframe the entire pilot story around session replay and checkout revenue loss.
Funnel analytics is a losing argument when the champion can't sell it upward. The sessions already wrote the business case: three replays of the same rage-click on the checkout page equals lost revenue waiting to be quantified. Build the business review around that finding. The evidence exists — the SE just needs to frame it.
Escalate Decision 2 of 3
Get the data team on a call before day 10 of the pilot.
Single-threaded deals at this stage lose at 2–3× the rate of multi-threaded ones. The champion cannot win a CTO conversation alone. Ask the champion to make the introduction — frame it as: "We want the business review to land for your whole team, not just for you."
Hold Decision 3 of 3
Decline the scope expansion request — explicitly and directly.
A scope expansion at 45 days is a delay signal or a confusion signal. Either way, adding scope dilutes the story. Say it clearly: "Let's nail the checkout finding first — that's your clearest path into the business review and the strongest proof point for the CTO."
WGO Signal Read — What the Framework Caught
"The customer said 'funnel analytics' but replayed the checkout rage-click session three times and never once opened the funnel builder. They told you what they need with their behavior, not their words. The pilot story was already written. The SE just needed to read it."

Customer Success Manager

The renewal that
looks safe — and isn't.

Three accounts. Three different risk profiles. Sixty-five days to renewal across all of them. The most dangerous account has the best usage numbers. The WGO reads the org chart the same way it reads a session funnel — it looks for who's missing.

⚠ New VP of Product never introduced — $310K account
⚠ DAU/MAU −18% + "budget season" twice + no exec contact
▲ 65 days to renewal — all three accounts
✓ Account B stable — lowest intervention needed
The Situation
Account A — $180K ARR
› DAU/MAU dropped 18% over 3 weeks. Champion mentioned "budget season" twice on the last call.
› No exec contact in 4 months.
Account B — $95K ARR
› Stable usage. QBR last week went well. Asked about a roadmap feature not yet GA.
Account C — $310K ARR
› Usage up 12%. Published a Fullstory case study internally. Champion is enthusiastic.
› Gong: a new VP of Product appeared — not on the original deal, has not been introduced to Fullstory.
View original WGO prompt
Prompt fed to WGO frameworkRead design-system/Worlds_Greatest_Operator.md and apply the WGO framework. Then: I'm a CSM with three renewals landing in 65 days. Apply the WGO CSM Playbook, the 30-90 day horizon model, and the Decision Quality Checklist. Give me: (1) the ranked risk order with your reasoning, (2) the one intervention per account that has the highest leverage, and (3) what I should NOT do this week. Account A ($180K): DAU/MAU dropped 18% in 3 weeks. Champion mentioned "budget season" twice. No exec contact in 4 months. Account B ($95K): Stable usage. Good QBR. Asked about a roadmap feature not yet GA. Account C ($310K): Usage up 12%. Published internal Fullstory case study. New VP of Product appeared in Gong — not on original deal, hasn't been introduced.
WGO Output — Triage Ranked by Risk
Highest Risk · $310K Account C
Introduce yourself to the new VP of Product this week — before the renewal conversation.
Good usage numbers don't protect against a stakeholder who didn't buy Fullstory and has no relationship with you. That VP has veto power. Have the champion make the intro. Lead with the internal case study they published — it's already their evidence, not yours.
High Risk · $180K Account A
Don't send an email. Call the champion and lead with what you already know.
"Budget season" twice in one call means a conversation is happening above the champion's level — without you in it. A generic check-in email signals that you don't know what's happening. Call directly: "I noticed usage patterns shifted — I want to make sure we're supporting the right things before your budget cycle closes."
Lower Risk · $95K Account B
Send a written roadmap acknowledgment. Nothing more.
A good QBR is goodwill to protect, not a signal that the renewal is secure. Respond to the feature request in writing — acknowledge it, share what you know today, commit to nothing. This is a trust-building move, not an escalation.
What NOT to do this week

Don't prioritize Account B just because the QBR felt good. Don't send Account A a "checking in" email — it signals you don't know what's happening. And don't assume Account C is safe because the usage dashboard is green. A new VP who has never heard of Fullstory is not a health signal — it's a gap.

WGO Signal Read — What the Framework Caught
"The most dangerous account had the best numbers. A new VP of Product who didn't buy the product and hasn't met you is a risk no usage dashboard will show. The WGO reads org charts the same way it reads session funnels — it looks for who's missing from the picture."

SE Manager

Five deals. Two SEs.
One decision that multiplies both.

A coverage decision that looks like a resource problem is actually a leverage problem. The WGO makes the coverage call, identifies the single point of failure, and finds the team asset hiding inside one of the deals.

⚠ SE on Deal 5 solo for 6 weeks — single point of failure
⚠ Deal 1 scope expansion is a delay or confusion signal
▲ $600K deal unassigned with a technical referral champion
✓ Deals 2 and 4 below SE investment threshold — remove from board
The Situation
Deal Inventory
1$400K · 45d — SE assigned, pilot strong. Customer requesting scope expansion outside pilot.
2$90K · 90d — No SE. AE requesting demo.
3$600K · 60d — No SE. Referral with technically sophisticated champion.
4$30K · 30d — No SE needed. AE can self-serve.
5$250K · 120d — SE solo for 6 weeks. Showing overload signs.
Team Profile
ASE A — Deep technical product knowledge. Less comfortable at exec level.
BSE B — Strong exec presence and storytelling. Building depth on funnel analytics.
View original WGO prompt
Prompt fed to WGO frameworkRead design-system/Worlds_Greatest_Operator.md and apply the WGO framework. Then: I'm an SE Manager. I have two SEs and five active deals. Using the WGO SE Manager Playbook, team enablement model, and Principle 4 (Efficiency · Effectiveness · Execution) — give me: coverage decision per deal, the one team leverage move this week, and what to document so the next cycle is faster. Deal 1: $400K, 45d, SE assigned, pilot strong, scope expansion requested outside pilot. Deal 2: $90K, 90d, no SE, AE wants demo. Deal 3: $600K, 60d, no SE, referral, technically sophisticated champion. Deal 4: $30K, 30d, no SE needed. Deal 5: $250K, 120d, SE solo 6 weeks, showing overload. SE A: Technical depth, weak exec storytelling. SE B: Strong exec presence, still building on funnel analytics.
WGO Output — Coverage + Leverage Decisions
Hold + Redirect Deal 1 · $400K
Keep the SE. Decline the scope expansion — explicitly.
A scope expansion at 45 days is a delay or confusion signal. Either way, expanding dilutes the win criteria and extends the cycle. Tell the AE: "We close on what we've already proven. Adding scope now moves the goalposts."
No SE — Remove from Board Deals 2 & 4
Both deals are below the SE investment threshold. AE self-serve.
$90K at 90 days and $30K at 30 days don't clear the bar for SE time at this stage. Clarity test applied: if removing SE involvement doesn't change the deal outcome, the SE should not be involved.
Assign SE B Deal 3 · $600K
SE B's exec presence outweighs the funnel analytics gap.
A referral deal with a technical champion means credibility is already established by the referral source. SE B needs to match that credibility at the executive level. Exec presence can't be faked in the room. Product depth gaps on funnel analytics can be prepped in advance.
Team Leverage Deal 5 + The System
Bring SE A into Deal 5 now. Build the reusable asset from Deal 1.
Six weeks solo on Deal 5 is a single point of failure — 120 days is enough runway to transition. Simultaneously: pull the checkout rage-click pattern from Deal 1's pilot into a reusable session evidence package. Tag it, write it up, share it. Both SEs get the asset. Every future deal benefits.
WGO Signal Read — What the Framework Caught
"The highest-leverage move this week isn't solving any individual deal. It's taking one insight from Deal 1 and turning it into an asset that makes both SEs more capable on every future deal. The WGO's job is to make the team compoundingly better — not to be the best SE in the room."
The WGO Manifesto
"
Most operators work in the present tense. The World's Greatest Operator lives in two tenses simultaneously — what needs to happen today, and what needs to be true in 90 days for today to have mattered.
Principle 2 — Pulse, Not Reaction
"
Customers tell you what they think they do. Sessions show you what they actually do. The gap between those two things is where the real decision lives.
Signal Source — Fullstory Sessions
"
The most important thing said on a sales call is usually the thing nobody wrote down. The WGO reads transcripts for what was quiet, not just what was loud.
Signal Source — Gong Transcripts
"
A to-do list is a comfort mechanism. It gives you the feeling of progress without the discomfort of judgment. The WGO trades that comfort for clarity — fewer items, higher stakes, cleaner outcomes.
Principle 1 — Few, High-Impact Decisions
"
The WGO's best week isn't the one where they closed the deal or saved the account. It's the one where they made someone else capable of doing both — without them in the room.
Principle 3 — Enable the Team
"
If you can't say the technical value in one sentence to a non-technical executive, you haven't decided yet. You've described your confusion in a longer format.
Clarity Test
"
The difference between a good operator and the greatest one isn't effort. It's the moment of recognition — seeing the pattern three weeks before everyone else does, and making the one move that changes what happens next.
